Welcome to the AIAP

In 2014, Professor Chandra K. Jaggi established the Association of Inventory Academicians and Practitioners (AIAP) in New Delhi, India. The primary goal of this organization is to create a dynamic forum that facilitates the exchange of knowledge and techniques among researchers, academicians, industry professionals, and scientists in the realm of Inventory Management and related disciplines. AIAP is committed to broadening horizons by fostering the sharing of insights and the application of innovative approaches. AIAP holds a prominent position as a society dedicated to the advancement of Inventory Control and Management in India. By promoting research, education, and practical implementations of inventory management principles, the society plays a pivotal role in enhancing both industrial practices and the theoretical foundations supporting the field. Through its unifying platform, AIAP brings together academicians and practitioners from all corners of the nation, thereby strengthening the collaborative efforts driving progress in the field of inventory management.

Our Mission:

  • Organizing Conferences, Seminars, Workshops: We aim to foster a culture of continuous learning and knowledge sharing by organizing events that facilitate discussions, presentations, and interactions among experts, researchers, and industry professionals. These gatherings promote the latest advancements and innovative solutions in inventory control and management.
  • Creating a Common Platform: Our society provides a common ground for Indian academicians and practitioners involved in inventory-related fields to connect, collaborate, and exchange ideas. Through this platform, we aim to encourage interdisciplinary dialogue and promote holistic growth.
  • Popularizing Inventory Education and Research: We strive to enhance the prominence of inventory control and management as crucial components in industrial, scientific, and technological problem-solving. By promoting teaching and research in this area, we contribute to sustainable development and optimized resource utilization.
  • Frontline Research and International Collaboration: Identifying key research areas and facilitating international collaboration are integral to our mission. We organize seminars, symposiums, and conferences to enable scholars and experts, especially from developing countries, to share insights and experiences, fostering a global perspective on inventory challenges.
  • Spreading Inventory Awareness: Our society extends its efforts to the masses by promoting inventory control techniques and methodologies. We believe that raising awareness about efficient inventory practices can lead to improved business operations and overall economic growth.
  • Expert Gatherings: We regularly organize gatherings of experts, practitioners, scientists, scholars, and students from diverse Indian academic institutions, research and development organizations, industries, and non-governmental organizations. These events aim to propagate the significance of inventory management and control in various sectors.
  • School of Quantitative Science: As part of our commitment to education and research, we aspire to establish a School of Quantitative Science. This institution will provide training, foster fundamental and applied research, and contribute to skill development and employment generation.
  • Reference Library: We are dedicated to creating a comprehensive reference library that houses a wealth of resources related to inventory control and management. This repository will serve as a valuable asset for researchers, practitioners, and students seeking in-depth knowledge in the field.

Core Principles:

  • Non-Profit Orientation: All our activities are carried out on a "Not for Profit" basis. Our primary goal is the advancement of knowledge, education, and research in the field of inventory control and management.
  • Ethical Utilization of Resources: The income, earnings, movable, and immovable properties of the society are solely directed towards the fulfillment of our stated objectives. No part of these resources is used for personal gain or profit.
